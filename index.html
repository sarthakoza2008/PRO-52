PANDA

    Intersting facts about pandas:-

  1. They Spend a Lot Of Their Day Eating

  2. A panda's paw has six digits—five fingers and an opposable pseudo-thumb.

  3. They sleep for 2-3 hours at a time, and then spend 3-4 hours eating.

  4. Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown) in colour.

CAMEL

    Intersting facts about camels:-

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

  2. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.

  3. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

  4. Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!

FLYING-CICADA

    Intersting facts about flying-cicada:-

  1. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.

  2. Cicadas are great at clinging to tree trunks and making loud screeching sounds by vibrating their bodies. But these bulky, red-eyed insects aren't so great at flying. The reason why may lie in the chemistry of their wings, a new study shows.

  3. Technically cicadas don't bite or sting; they do however pierce and suck. They might try to pierce and suck you, but don't worry, they aren't Vampires nor are they malicious or angry — they're just ignorant and think you're a tree. Drinking human blood would probably kill a cicada.

  4. The body of a cicada is similar to that of a violin or a guitar, in that much of it consists of empty, air-filled spaces that act like a resonating chamber and amplify the sound they generate.

CHAMELEONS

Intersting facts about chameleons:-

  1. Chameleons are a very unique branch of the lizard group of reptiles.

  2. There are around 160 species of chameleon.

  3. Chameleons live in warm varied habitats from rainforests through to deserts.

  4. Changing skin colour is an important part of communication among chameleon.

PIGEON

    Intersting facts about pigeons:-

  1. pigeons are incredibly intelligent and complex animals.

  2. pigeons are also good at multitasking.

  3. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

  4. An exception is the white domestic pigeon, the symbol known as the "dove of peace".

TEDDY-BEAR

    Intersting facts about teddybear:-

  1. The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.

  2. On February 15, 1903, toy store owner and inventor Morris Michtom places two stuffed bears in his shop window, advertising them as Teddy bears. Michtom had earlier petitioned President Theodore Roosevelt for permission to use his nickname, Teddy.

  3. The first teddies were actually only made in 1902, and at Pollock's Toy Museum near Goodge Street.

  4. A teddy bear is a stuffed toy in the form of a bear.
